## Problem Statement

ShoreExplorer has **zero organic traffic**. Without users, every other revenue stream is irrelevant. We need a multi-channel strategy to bring cruise passengers to the site, with a focus on **free/low-cost methods** that compound over time.

## Channel 1: SEO & Port Guide Content (Highest Priority)

### Target Keywords

**High-intent, moderate-competition keywords:**

| Keyword Pattern | Monthly Search Volume (est.) | Competition |
|---|---|---|
| "things to do in [port] from cruise ship" | 500–2,000 | Low–Medium |
| "[port] shore excursion tips" | 100–500 | Low |
| "best activities [port] cruise port" | 200–1,000 | Medium |
| "[port] cruise port walking guide" | 100–500 | Low |
| "what to do in [port] in one day" | 500–3,000 | Medium |
| "[port] cruise terminal to city" | 200–800 | Low |
| "free things to do in [port] cruise" | 200–1,000 | Low |

### On-Page SEO Requirements

For each port guide page:

1. **Title tag:** `[Port Name] Shore Excursion Guide — Top Activities & Tips | ShoreExplorer`
2. **Meta description:** `Plan your perfect day in [Port]. Discover the best activities, local food, and transport tips from the cruise terminal. Free AI-powered day planner.`
3. **H1:** `[Port Name] Cruise Port Day Guide`
4. **Content structure:**
   - Port overview (200–300 words)
   - Getting from the terminal to town (transport options, costs, times)
   - Top 10 activities (each with photo, description, cost, booking link)
   - Local food & drink recommendations (3–5 options)
   - Weather by month (table)
   - Safety tips
   - CTA: "Generate Your Personalized Day Plan" button
5. **Schema markup:** `TravelAction`, `TouristAttraction`, `FAQPage`
6. **Internal linking:** Link to related ports, related trip planning tools
7. **Images:** Optimized, with descriptive alt text, lazy loaded
8. **Word count:** 1,500–2,500 words per port guide

### Technical SEO Checklist

- [ ] Add `sitemap.xml` generation for all port guide pages
- [ ] Add `robots.txt` with proper directives
- [ ] Implement canonical URLs for all pages
- [ ] Add Open Graph and Twitter Card meta tags
- [ ] Ensure all pages load in <3 seconds (Core Web Vitals)
- [ ] Add structured data (JSON-LD) for tourist attractions
- [ ] Submit sitemap to Google Search Console
- [ ] Set up Google Search Console monitoring
- [ ] Implement breadcrumb navigation
- [ ] Add internal linking between port guides and trip planner

## Channel 2: Cruise Forum Engagement

### Target Forums

1. **Cruise Critic** (cruisecritic.com) — The largest cruise community
   - Create a profile, contribute genuinely helpful port tips
   - Add ShoreExplorer link in signature after earning credibility
   - Answer questions in "Ports of Call" forums
   - Share port guides when genuinely relevant to a discussion

2. **Reddit** (/r/Cruise, /r/CruiseShips, /r/travel)
   - Share useful port guides as Reddit posts
   - Answer cruise planning questions
   - Create "I built a free cruise port planner" launch post
   - Be authentic — Reddit detects and punishes self-promotion

3. **Facebook Groups**
   - "Cruise Addicts" (500K+ members)
   - "Cruise Ship Crew and Passengers"
   - Ship-specific groups (Royal Caribbean, Carnival, etc.)
   - Share helpful tips first, build trust, then mention the tool

### Rules of Engagement

- **80/20 rule:** 80% genuine help, 20% mentioning ShoreExplorer
- Never spam links — contribute to discussions first
- When mentioning ShoreExplorer, frame it as "I found this helpful" not "use my app"
- Track which forums drive the most traffic (UTM parameters)

## Channel 3: Social Media Strategy

### TikTok (@ShoreExplorer)

**Content pillars:**
1. **"60 Seconds in [Port]"** — Quick visual guides to cruise ports
2. **"Don't Make This Mistake in [Port]"** — Common tourist traps and tips
3. **"What £50 Gets You in [Port]"** — Budget activity showcases
4. **"Free Things to Do in [Port]"** — High-value, low-cost activities
5. **"AI Planned My Day"** — Show ShoreExplorer generating a plan in real time

**Posting schedule:** 3–5 videos/week
**Goal:** 10K followers in 3 months

### Instagram (@ShoreExplorer)

**Content pillars:**
1. **Port photography** — Beautiful cruise port images (use free stock or user-submitted)
2. **Carousel guides** — "Top 5 Things to Do in Barcelona" (swipeable)
3. **Stories** — Poll followers on their favourite ports, cruise tips
4. **Reels** — Repurpose TikTok content

**Posting schedule:** 5 posts/week + daily stories
**Goal:** 5K followers in 3 months

### Pinterest (@ShoreExplorer)

**Why Pinterest:** Extremely effective for travel content. Pins have a long shelf life (6+ months) and drive consistent traffic.

**Content:**
1. Create pin-worthy images for each port guide
2. Design infographics: "Barcelona Cruise Port: What to Know"
3. Create boards: "Mediterranean Cruise Ports", "Caribbean Shore Excursions", etc.
4. Link all pins back to port guide pages

**Posting schedule:** 10–15 pins/week (can be automated with Tailwind)
**Goal:** 1K monthly views in 3 months

## Channel 4: Travel Blogger Outreach

### Strategy

1. **Identify 50 cruise travel bloggers** with 1K–50K followers
2. **Offer them:**
   - Free mention in port guides (backlink exchange)
   - Featured "Guest Expert Tips" section in port guides
   - Early access to premium features
   - Revenue share on affiliate conversions from their traffic
3. **Ask them to:**
   - Write a review/mention of ShoreExplorer
   - Include a link in their "cruise planning tools" posts
   - Share on social media

### Outreach Template

```
Subject: Free cruise port planning tool for your readers

Hi [Name],

I'm building ShoreExplorer, a free AI-powered cruise port day planner. 
I think your readers who plan shore excursions would find it genuinely useful.

The tool generates personalized day plans based on your schedule, preferences, 
and weather — with interactive maps and booking links.

Would you be interested in:
- Trying it out and sharing your thoughts with your audience?
- Being featured as a "Port Expert" in our [Port Name] guide?

No pressure — I just think it could be a valuable resource for cruise 
travelers and wanted to share it with creators in the space.

Best,
[Name]
```

## Channel 5: Google Ads (Paid, Low Budget)

### Campaign Structure

**Budget:** £100–200/month (start small, scale what works)

**Campaign 1: Search Ads**
- Target keywords: "cruise port planner", "shore excursion planning app", "things to do at [port] cruise"
- Bid strategy: Manual CPC, starting at £0.20–0.50
- Landing page: Port guide pages (relevant to search term)
- Expected CTR: 3–5%
- Expected CPC: £0.30–0.80

**Campaign 2: Display Ads (retargeting)**
- Retarget users who visited ShoreExplorer but didn't generate a plan
- Budget: £30–50/month
- Show on cruise-related websites

### ROI Tracking

- Track cost per acquired user
- Track revenue per user (ads + affiliate + premium)
- Target: Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) < £2
- Target: Lifetime Value (LTV) > £5
